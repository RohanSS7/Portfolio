Evaluation of In-Real-Life factors as a dimension of Source Credibility

Final year undergraduate management dissertation titled "An Evaluation of the Effectiveness of In-Real-Life factors as a Source Credibility Dimension for Celebrity Endorsement Marketing and its Influence on Consumers Purchase Decisions: A look into Sports Celebrity Endorsers."

My Dissertation Journey

As part of my academic journey, I completed a dissertation titled “An Evaluation of the Effectiveness of In-Real-Life Factors as a Source Credibility Dimension for Celebrity Endorsement Marketing and Its Influence on Consumers’ Purchase Decisions: A Focus on Sports Celebrity Endorsers.” This research was an exciting opportunity to dive deep into the intersection of marketing, consumer psychology, and the world of sports.

Why This Topic?

Celebrity endorsements are everywhere, especially in sports marketing, where athletes influence consumer behavior and shape brand perceptions. While much research focuses on a celebrity’s trustworthiness, expertise, and attractiveness, I noticed a gap—how personal life events and off-field behavior affect their credibility and, in turn, consumer purchase decisions. My goal was to bridge this gap by exploring the impact of these “in-real-life” factors.

The Research Process

I adopted a mixed-method approach, combining interviews with sports enthusiasts and a survey of over 120 respondents. Through thematic and statistical analyses, I unearthed fascinating insights: (1)Consumers highly value a sports celebrity’s personal traits and behavior, seeing these as crucial to their overall credibility. (2)Positive factors, like humility and fan engagement, boosted trust, while scandals and unethical behavior significantly damaged perceptions. (3)Importantly, these real-life factors often outweighed traditional attributes like expertise and attractiveness in shaping purchase decisions.

Key Findings and Implications

The study revealed that marketers need to go beyond the surface when choosing celebrity endorsers. A celebrity’s off-field image can make or break a campaign, influencing not just product perception but also the brand’s reputation. These findings are especially relevant in today’s connected world, where every action is amplified by social media.
